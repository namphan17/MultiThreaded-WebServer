Tim Berners-Lee

In 1980, Tim Berners-Lee proposed this amazing idea for a project that used hypertext to enabel sharing and updating information among researchers. Tim created ENQUIRE, a very early hypertext database system that worked much like a Wiki. This, however, was only the first protoype. In 1989, Tim, who was now working as a scientist at the European Organization for Nuclear Research, created another prototype for another hypertext project. This project was developed as CERN needed to be able to share information quickly and simply among physicists and other academic institutions. He called his project "WorldWideWeb". Berners-Lee is now the director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).

Some other information about Tim Berners-Lee

  • Elected to the World Wide Web Hall of Fame in 1994
  • He was knighted by Queen Elizabath II in 2004
  • He was elected a foreign associate of the United States National Academy of Sciences
  • Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame by the Internet Society in 2012
