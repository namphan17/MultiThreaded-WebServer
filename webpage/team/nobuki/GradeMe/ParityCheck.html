Parity Check

  • bit-level error detection: detecting the corruption of bits in a linked-layer frame sent from one node to another physically connected neighboring node.
  • Parity Checks: the simplest form of detection is the use of a single parity bit.
  • If the information has D bits, an even parity scheme will send the total number of 1s in the d +1bits.
  • An odd scheme will chose a value with an odd number of 1s.
  • There is a 50 percent chance method
  • one error can easily be canceled out by another error
  • Two-dimensional parity scheme both the row and column containing the flipped bit will be in error.
  • This makes the chance of having an error in the check much lower along with knowing where the error is.
  • FEC or forwarding error correction is the ability of the receiver to both detect and correct errors.
  • Thses techniques are usually used in audio storage or playback devices.
