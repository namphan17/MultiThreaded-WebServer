Ethernet

  • The ethernet has taken over the wired LAN market.
  • There are many reasons to the Ethernet's success
    • It was developed early, so network admins became familiar with the Ethernet.
    • Also, its competitors were more complex and expensive than the ethernet.
    • For these two reasons, the admins were reluctant to switch over to other ways to set up LAN
    • Ethernet had lower data rates than the newere technology, but Ethernet updated itself to keep up with the data rates.
    • Switched ethernet was also created.
  • Bob Metcalfe and David Boggs invented the ethernet in the mid 1970s
  • It originally used a coaxial bus to internconnect the nodes, which lasted until the mid 1990s.
  • It switched to using a hub, which just recreates bits that arrived on one end and out another.
  • In the 2000s the center hub was replaced with a switch that increased the data rates.
  • That switch was collision-less and a bona-fide store-and-forward packet switch that only operated through layer 2
  • This is different that routers that opperate through layer 3
  • Frame structure parts and their size
    • Data field 46-1,500 bytes
    • Destination address 6 bytes
    • Source address 6 bytes
    • Type field 2bytes
    • Cyclic redundancy check 4 bytes
    • Preamble 8 bytes
