Term Definition
1. Packet Switching a. A form of data transmission which allocates a specific amount of bandwidth to form a continuous line of communication between end users.
2. Circuit Switching b. A form of multiplexing which allocates a specific bandwidth for each connection.
3. Frequency-Division Multiplexing c. A form of data transmission which splits up messages into byte sized parts and transmits them piecemeal through nodes in a network.
4. Time-Division Multiplexing 3. A form of multiplexing which rapidly cycles through each connection using the entire bandwidth.
